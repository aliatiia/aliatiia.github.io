      The theoretical component of my research relates the computational complexity law to the evolution of topological properties in biological networks. The experimental component involves experimental methods in the areas of DNA/RNA computing nanotechnology.



      The evolution of topology in biological networks      		 Cells come under evolutionary pressure to alter what, when and whether some genes should be expressed at a given moment in their life cycle. A gene can be assigned benefit/damage scores denoting the degree to which its expression provides (dis)advantage to the organism under said pressure. A gene may therefore be an asset (benefit > 0 and damage = 0), a liability (b = 0, d > 0), or both (b > 0, d > 0). What subset of genes should be deleted/mutated, and which should be conserved, such that the total number of damaging interactions is minimized to a tolerable threshold?
